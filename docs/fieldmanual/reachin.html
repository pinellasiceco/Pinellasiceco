❄️ Reach-In Cooler Service

Cleaning, Sanitization & ATP Verification Protocol
📋 Service Overview ▼

Reach-in coolers are serviced on the same visit as the ice machine. The service is a thorough surface clean and ATP verification — no disassembly required. Typical time: 15–20 minutes per unit, up to 3 units.

Primary contamination sites:

  • 1 Door gaskets — most critical. Inspectors check these first. Contamination lives in the folds.
  • 2 Interior walls and shelves — food residue and biofilm accumulate on all surfaces.
  • 3 Drain pan — most neglected, often most contaminated. Standing organic material.
ATP Target Below 100 RLU post-clean. More lenient than ice machines — cooler surfaces store food, not produce it — but sub-100 is the defensible standard.
🧰 Tools & Supplies Checklist ▼
  • ATP luminometer (same as ice machine service)
  • ATP swabs — 6 to 10 per visit (more than ice machine)
  • Quaternary ammonium sanitizer spray (200–400 ppm)
  • Spray bottle
  • Microfiber cloths — clean, designated for this service only
  • Stiff-bristle brush (toothbrush-style) for gasket folds
  • Nitrile gloves
  • Phone/camera for documentation
🟡 Step-by-Step Protocol ▼
1
Pre-clean ATP test
Before touching anything, take a baseline reading. Swab the door gasket fold on the most-used door — typically the most contaminated surface and gives the most dramatic before/after comparison. Record the pre reading. Photograph the luminometer screen with the cooler door visible in frame.
2
Photo documentation
Photograph gasket condition before cleaning. Capture any visible mold, discoloration, or buildup. Also photograph the interior if shelves or walls show debris or residue. This is your proof of condition.
3
Gasket cleaning — most important step
Spray quaternary ammonium sanitizer directly into the gasket folds. Work solution into folds with the stiff-bristle brush — every crease, all four sides. Wipe with clean microfiber cloth. Repeat until cloth comes away clean. Heavily contaminated gaskets may need 2–3 passes. Check all gaskets on all doors.
4
Interior surface cleaning
Spray sanitizer on interior walls, shelves, and drain pan. Wipe top to bottom — never bottom to top. Pay attention to shelf brackets and plastic components where residue accumulates. The drain pan is frequently the most neglected — do not skip it.
5
Post-clean ATP test
Wait 60 seconds after cleaning. Swab the same location as the pre-clean test. Record the post reading. Target: below 100 RLU. Photograph the reading. If above 100, see Referral Triggers below.
6
Document in app
Log service visit with reach-in data:
  • Overall result (pass/fail)
  • Pre and post RLU per unit
  • Gasket condition rating per unit
  • Notes on any referrals needed
🧪 ATP Reference ▼
Reading Status Action
< 10 RLU Excellent Document and certify
10–100 RLU Pass Document and certify
101–300 RLU Marginal Re-clean and retest
> 300 RLU Fail Referral likely needed
Note on Threshold Reach-in cooler threshold is 100 RLU — vs 10 RLU for ice machines. Ice is consumed directly; cooler surfaces store packaged food. Higher threshold is still a defensible, documented standard.
🔍 Gasket Condition Rating ▼
Rating Description Action
✅ Good Supple, no cracks, seals fully Clean and sanitize
⚠️ Fair Minor surface cracks, still seals Clean + note for monitoring
🔶 Poor Deep cracks or pulling away from frame Clean + referral for replacement
❌ Failed Torn or missing sections Referral before next service
Poor or Failed Gaskets These are referrals to a refrigeration tech — they cannot be fully sanitized and will continue to fail ATP regardless of cleaning quality. Log the gasket rating for each unit in service notes.
💬 What to Tell the Client ▼
When result is PASS

"We cleaned and sanitized all the door gaskets and interior surfaces on your reach-in coolers. Pre reading was [X] RLU, post reading is [Y] — that's a pass. Your compliance report includes both readings. Keep this on file for your next inspection."

When result is MARGINAL (re-clean needed)

"First pass brought it down but not quite where we want it. Give me another few minutes — I want to do another pass on the gaskets and retest before I sign off on this."

When gasket needs replacement

"The post reading is still elevated. The gasket itself is cracked — the contamination is living inside the damaged rubber and we can't sanitize it out. You'll need a refrigeration tech to replace this gasket. I can connect you with someone. Once it's replaced, we'll get a clean reading no problem."

When result is PASS but gasket is Fair

"Everything passed today — reading came in at [Y]. One thing to watch: the gasket on unit [X] is starting to show some wear. It's not a problem yet but it's worth replacing in the next few months before it becomes one. I'll note it in your report."

🔴 Referral Triggers ▼
Flag for Refrigeration Tech Referral These are not Pinellas Ice Co services. Flagging them and making the referral is part of the value you provide. Log all referrals in service notes.
  • ✕ Gasket rated Poor or Failed
  • ✕ Unit not maintaining temperature
  • ✕ Excessive ice buildup on interior coils
  • ✕ Drain pan not draining / standing water inside
  • ✕ Door not sealing properly — close door on a piece of paper, it should have resistance when you pull it out
  • ✕ Unusual sounds or compressor cycling issues
