🚫 When to Refer Out — Universal Rules ▼
Stop and refer out if you encounter any of the following:
  • Refrigerant leak — oily residue near fittings, frost patterns, hissing sound. Do not operate. Refer to licensed HVAC/R technician immediately.
  • Compressor failure — compressor not starting, loud knocking, trips breaker. Refer out — this is beyond PM scope.
  • Electrical issues — burned wiring, tripped GFCI that won't reset, scorched control board, burning smell. Refer to senior tech or licensed electrician.
  • Water inlet valve failure — valve not opening or closing, continuous water flow, flooding. Refer out if valve replacement required.
  • Control board errors — SERVICE fault, multiple repeating error codes after reset, unresponsive display. Refer to senior tech.
  • Structural damage — cracked evaporator, damaged bin, bent or broken components that affect food safety. Document and refer out.
  • Active mold (black/green) — visible mold beyond normal pink slime. Document with photos, sanitize what you can, escalate for remediation protocol.
  • Rodent or pest evidence — droppings, nesting, chewed wiring. Halt service, document with photos, notify manager and refer to pest control first.
  • Customer requests repair beyond PM scope — any repair requiring parts, refrigerant, or electrical work is outside PM scope. Quote separately or refer to senior tech.
💧 Everpure Filter Replacement EV9612-22 ▼
When to replace Replace every 6 months or when the capacity limit is reached — whichever comes first. Always check the installation date marked on the housing.
1
Turn off the water supply. Locate the shut-off valve on the supply line feeding the filter head and close it completely.
2
Relieve line pressure. Press the red pressure relief button on the filter head (if present) or slowly loosen the sump to bleed pressure. Have a towel ready.
3
Remove the old filter. Turn the filter cartridge counterclockwise (left) and pull straight down. Dispose of properly — do not attempt to clean or reuse.
4
Inspect the filter head. Wipe the head gasket seat with a clean damp cloth. Check the O-ring or gasket — replace if cracked, flattened, or missing.
5
Install the new EV9612-22 filter. Push up and turn clockwise until snug. Do not overtighten — hand-tight plus a quarter turn is sufficient.
6
Turn water supply back on slowly. Open the supply valve gradually and check immediately for leaks at the filter head connection.
7
Flush the filter for 5 minutes. Allow water to flow through the system continuously for a full 5 minutes to purge carbon fines and activate the filter media.
8
Check for leaks a second time. After flushing, inspect all connections again with the system pressurized. Dry any wet surfaces and watch for drips.
9
Mark the installation date. Write the date clearly on the new filter housing with a permanent marker. Record in your service notes.
🔌 Drain Plug Checklist ▼
Critical — Never Skip A loose drain plug = machine drains during operation = no ice = callback from a frustrated client.
  • Before cleaning: Remove the drain plug and allow the sump/reservoir to fully drain before adding cleaner solution.
  • During cleaning: Keep drain plug out while circulating cleaner. Never leave the machine unattended while draining.
  • Before sanitizing: Reinstall the drain plug securely before adding sanitizer solution. Confirm it is fully seated and locked.
  • Before restart: Do a final visual check — drain plug is in, tight, and the drain line is clear. Only then restart the machine.
🛡️ Universal Safety Rules ▼
  • Wear nitrile gloves at all times when handling cleaner or sanitizer chemicals. Both products can cause skin and eye irritation. If splash occurs, flush with water immediately.
  • Never mix chemicals. Cleaner and sanitizer are used sequentially, never together. Do not add any other products to the machine. Do not use bleach.
  • Photograph before you touch anything. Document initial condition, any error codes showing on the display, and any visible contamination. This protects you and the company.
  • Never operate a machine you believe has a refrigerant leak. Oil staining around fittings, abnormal frost patterns, or hissing sounds are stop-work conditions. Tag the unit and refer out.
  • Leave the machine cleaner than you found it — or don't restart it. If conditions are unsafe (active pests, severe mold, electrical hazard), document thoroughly, do not restart the machine, and notify your manager before leaving the site.
