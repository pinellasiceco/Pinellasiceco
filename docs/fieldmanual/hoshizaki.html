HOSHIZAKI

KM Crescent · DCM Nugget · FM Flaker
⚡ Quick Facts ▼

Market share: ~30% — most common brand you'll encounter.

Found in: Restaurants, bars, hotels throughout Tampa Bay.

Key difference: Stainless steel evaporator, more scale-resistant than competitors. Cleaning mode (WASH) is the simplest of all brands — hold one button.

📋 Identify This Machine ▼

The ice is crescent-shaped (half-moon). Model sticker is inside the front panel — lift up and pull out, no tools needed. The number after "KM-" is the approximate daily ice production in pounds.

KM series = crescent cube (most common)

DCM series = nugget/cubelet dispenser

FM series = flaker

What's Inside

When you open the front panel you'll see:

  • The evaporator — large stainless steel component in upper section, where ice forms. Looks like a textured metal plate.
  • The water trough — white plastic reservoir at the bottom, where you pour cleaning solution.
  • The water curtain — plastic flap that hangs between evaporator and bin. Slides straight out.
  • The ice deflector — angled plastic piece above the bin opening.
  • The bin — lower section where ice collects.
  • The drain plug — rubber plug at lowest point of the sump.
  • The air filter — flat foam panel on front or side.
🔵 Before You Start — KM Series ▼
1
Tell the owner ice production will stop for 1.5–3 hours.
2
Locate the power switch — front panel, behind the access door.
3
Locate the water supply valve — behind the machine on the wall.
4
Have all your supplies ready before opening the machine.
🟡 Deep Clean — KM Series ▼
Phase 1 — Document Before Touching Anything
1
Open the front panel. Lift up and pull out — no tools needed on most KM models.
2
Take photos before touching anything. Photograph:
  • The inside of the machine from the front (wide shot)
  • The water trough (the white plastic reservoir at the bottom)
  • The evaporator surface (the metal component up top)
  • The bin walls — look for pink slime, black mold, mineral deposits
  • The bin floor and drain area
  • The underside of the water curtain
3
Put your gloves on.
4
Take pre-clean ATP swabs — do ALL of these before cleaning anything, and record each reading in the app:
Swab 1 — Water trough interior Reach into the trough with your Ultrasnap swab. Press firmly against the floor and walls. Z-pattern. 4 full seconds of contact. This is almost always your highest reading. Hand the luminometer to the owner and let them read the number themselves.
Swab 2 — Underside of the water curtain The plastic curtain that hangs between the evaporator and bin. Swab the underside surface — the dark side facing downward.
Swab 3 — Ice scoop handle If there's an ice scoop, swab the entire handle. Owners touch this constantly and almost never sanitize it.
Swab 4 — Bin drain area The floor of the bin around the drain plug. Press firmly.
Swab 5 — Float valve housing The plastic housing for the water level float, in the trough area. Swab accessible surfaces.
5
Record all readings in the app before doing anything else. Photograph the luminometer display for any reading above 500 RLU.
Phase 2 — Prepare the Machine
6
Scoop all ice out of the bin and throw it away. Every single piece. Do not leave any ice in the machine during cleaning.
7
Enter WASH mode. Press and hold the WASH button for 3–5 seconds until the machine beeps and the WASH indicator light comes on.
8
The machine will harvest any ice remaining on the evaporator. You will hear it — ice dropping into the bin. This takes 2–4 minutes. Wait for it to finish completely.
9
Scoop out and discard this harvested ice too.
10
The machine will run a pump-out cycle, draining the water trough. You'll hear water draining. Wait until the draining sound stops completely — about 2–3 minutes.
11
Look into the trough and confirm it is empty. Do not add chemicals until the trough is fully drained.
Phase 3 — First Cleaning Cycle
12
Mix your cleaning solution for a deep clean:
  • 4 oz Nu-Calgon Nickel-Safe Cleaner
  • 16 oz clean water
  • Mix in your bucket before adding to the machine
  • Make 32 oz total (8 oz cleaner + 24 oz water) for trough plus manual cleaning
13
Pour the mixed solution into the water trough.
14
Press the WASH button again to start the cleaning cycle. The pump will start circulating the solution. You will hear it running.
15
Set a timer for 20 minutes. Go to Phase 4 now.
Phase 4 — Manual Cleaning While Cycle Runs
Tip Work top to bottom. You have 20 minutes.
16
Remove the water curtain. It slides straight out on most KM models — grip it and pull toward you. Lay it in your bucket with some diluted cleaning solution to soak.
17
Remove the ice deflector — the angled plastic piece above the bin opening. It usually clips out. Soak it with the curtain.
18
Scrub the evaporator surface. The stainless steel component visible now that the curtain is removed. Use your soft brush and cleaning solution. Light pressure — you're helping the cleaner contact scale deposits, not scrubbing a floor.
19
Spray the bin walls with diluted cleaning solution from your spray bottle. Scrub with your brush — top to bottom, all four walls. Pay extra attention to:
  • Corners where walls meet (biofilm loves corners)
  • Any pink or orange discoloration (Serratia marcescens — very common, completely normal, important to clean)
  • The waterline where ice has been sitting
  • Any black spots (mold — scrub thoroughly)
20
Scrub the bin floor. Focus on the area around the drain — organic material concentrates there.
21
Scrub the bin door and gasket. Look closely at the gasket folds — mold grows there. Get your brush into every fold.
22
Scrub the ice chute — the passage where ice falls into the bin.
23
Remove the drain plug (it's on a chain or tether). Scrub around the drain opening and clean the plug itself. Set it aside.
24
Remove the air filter — slides or lifts out. Rinse under running water until completely clear. Set aside to dry completely.
25
Take the curtain and deflector out of the soaking solution. Scrub both sides of each. Rinse with clean water and inspect — scrub again if biofilm is still visible.
Phase 5 — Assess: Does It Need a Second Cleaning Cycle?
26
When the 20-minute timer goes off, check these before deciding whether to proceed to rinsing:
  • Is there still visible white or gray scale on the trough walls or bottom?
  • Is there still visible white or gray deposits on the evaporator?
  • Is there still visible biofilm on the bin walls?
Important If YES to any of these: Mix a fresh batch of cleaning solution at the same 1:4 concentration. Pour into trough. Press WASH to run a second cycle. This is common on first visits. It adds 20–30 minutes but is necessary. Do not skip this if scale or biofilm is still visible.

If NO to all: Proceed to Phase 6.

Phase 6 — Rinse Cycles
27
Press WASH to drain the cleaning solution from the trough. Wait for complete drain.
28
Reinstall the drain plug before adding rinse water. Push in firmly. Tug it to confirm it won't come out.
29
Add clean water to the trough. Press WASH to run a rinse cycle. Run for 10 minutes. Drain completely.
30
Add clean water again. Run another 10-minute rinse. Drain.
31
Add clean water again. Run a third 10-minute rinse. Drain.
Tip Three rinse cycles minimum on a deep clean. After the third rinse the water draining out should be completely clear with no foam. If still cloudy or foamy, run a fourth rinse.
Phase 7 — Sanitizer Application
32
Mix Nu-Calgon No-Rinse Sanitizer: 0.5 oz sanitizer per 1 gallon of clean water. Mix in your spray bottle. This is a NO-RINSE product. Do not rinse after applying. Rinsing it off defeats the entire purpose.
33
Spray all internal surfaces:
  • All bin walls — spray until visibly wet
  • Bin floor and drain area
  • Ice chute
  • Water trough walls and floor
  • Evaporator surface
  • Both sides of the water curtain
  • Both sides of the ice deflector
34
Pour approximately 4–6 oz of mixed sanitizer solution directly into the trough.
35
DO NOT RINSE. Leave the sanitizer on every surface.
36
Let air dry for 5 minutes. Use this time to clean up your work area and rinse your brush and bucket.
Phase 8 — Reassemble
37
Reinstall the water curtain. It should sit level with no gaps. Confirm it's seated correctly.
38
Reinstall the ice deflector. Confirm it clicks or seats into position.
39
Reinstall the drain plug. Push firmly and turn to lock. CRITICAL: Tug on it firmly after installation. If it comes out, the machine will drain improperly during operation and produce no ice.
40
The air filter — only reinstall it if it is completely dry. If still damp, wait. A wet filter blocks airflow and causes overheating. When dry, slide or clip it back into position.
41
Close the front panel.
Phase 9 — Restart and Verify
42
Press the ICE button to return to normal operation.
43
The machine will begin its startup cycle. Allow 15–20 minutes before the first harvest. You will hear the compressor running.
44
When the first harvest drops into the bin — throw it away. All of it. The first batch may have residual cleaner.
45
Wait for the second harvest.
46
Take post-clean ATP swabs:
Swab 1 — Water trough interior Same location as pre-clean. Target: under 10 RLU.
Swab 2 — Evaporator plate surface Center of plate. Target: under 10 RLU.
Swab 3 — Bin wall Mid-height. Target: under 10 RLU.
47
If any reading is above 30 RLU: Re-spray that surface with sanitizer solution. Wait 5 minutes. Retest. If still above 30 after second application — document it and note it to the owner. A surface that won't come down may have pitting or damage harboring bacteria.
48
Take post-clean photos of the same locations as pre-clean.
49
Log everything in the app — all ATP readings, notes, photos.
50
Show the owner the before and after numbers. "Before we started, your ice machine water contact surface read [X] RLU. The FDA standard is 10. After our service it's at [Y]. That's a [Z]% reduction. Here's your compliance documentation."
🟢 Routine Maintenance — KM Series ▼
1
Open front panel. Take photos. Take pre-clean ATP swabs (water trough and curtain underside minimum). Record readings.
2
If any reading is above 400 RLU on a machine you've previously cleaned — upgrade this to a deep clean.
3
Scoop and discard all ice from bin.
4
Press and hold WASH button. Wait for harvest and drain.
5
Mix lighter cleaning solution: 2 oz cleaner per 16 oz water.
6
Pour into trough. Press WASH to start cycle. Set 20-min timer.
7
While cycle runs:
  • Remove curtain — wipe both sides with cleaner solution on cloth. Rinse curtain. Set aside.
  • Remove deflector — wipe with cleaner solution. Rinse. Set aside.
  • Wipe bin walls with cleaning solution cloth — no heavy scrubbing needed if machine was clean from last visit.
  • Wipe bin floor and drain area.
  • Wipe bin door gasket folds.
  • Wipe ice chute.
  • Remove air filter — rinse if dusty, check condition.
8
When cycle ends, drain.
9
Run 2 rinse cycles — add clean water, run 10 minutes each, drain completely after each.
10
Apply sanitizer to all surfaces. No rinse. Air dry 5 minutes.
11
Reinstall curtain, deflector, drain plug (confirm seated), air filter (confirm dry).
12
Return to ICE mode. Wait for first harvest. Discard it.
13
Post-clean ATP (trough and evaporator). Record. Photograph. Log in app.
Filter Replacement If filter replacement is due (every 6 months on monthly plan): Complete during Step 9 while rinse cycles run.

🔧 Common Problems ▼
Heavy Scale on Evaporator
What it looks like

White or gray crusty deposits on the stainless evaporator. Can range from a light film to thick hard buildup that looks almost like concrete.

What to do

Use deep clean concentration (1:4). Extend cleaning cycle to 40–45 minutes. Gently scrub with soft brush during cycle. If scale persists after first cycle — run a second cycle with fresh solution.

Refer out if

Scale is so thick that evaporator fins are visibly blocked or the evaporator surface appears damaged.

Pink or Orange Slime on Bin Walls
What it is

Serratia marcescens — airborne bacteria that thrives in cool wet environments. Extremely common in Florida. A health code violation if an inspector finds it.

What to tell the owner

"This is very common — almost every machine I service in Florida has some degree of this. It's airborne bacteria that settles in the bin. That's exactly why regular cleaning and documentation matters."

What NOT to say

Don't say "contaminated" or "dangerous" — it causes alarm and defensiveness.

What to do

Standard cleaning handles this completely. Document with photos before cleaning.

Black Mold on Curtain or Gaskets
What it looks like

Black spots on the plastic water curtain or door gasket.

What to do

Scrub with cleaning solution. If mold is deeply embedded in gasket material — note it and recommend replacement.

Refer out if

Gasket is visibly cracked, torn, or mold has penetrated deeply.

Bin Full Sensor Triggering When Bin Isn't Full
What it looks like

Machine stops producing ice but bin has room.

What to do

Check for ice buildup near the sensor arm. Clear any ice blocking it. If sensor appears damaged or corroded, refer out.

Machine Producing Cloudy or Off-Tasting Ice After Cleaning
What it means

Usually residual cleaner from insufficient rinsing.

What to do

Run additional rinse cycles and discard more ice. If persistent — refer out for water quality assessment.

⚠️ Error Codes ▼

How to read Hoshizaki error codes: The control panel LEDs flash in a pattern. Count the flashes carefully. The number tells you the fault.

Code Meaning Your Action
F1 Harvest failure — taking too long to release ice Note, photograph, refer out. Do not clean.
F2 Long freeze cycle — taking too long to make ice Note, photograph, refer out. Do not clean.
F3 Water supply issue — not enough water Check water valve (turn fully counter-clockwise). Replace filter if clogged. If still showing F3, refer out.
F4 Drainage issue — water not draining Check drain plug is seated. Check drain line for kinks. If clear and F4 persists, refer out.
F5 High temperature — machine overheating Check and clean air filter. Check clearance (6" sides, 12" top). If clean and clear, refer out.
F8 Control board fault Power cycle (off 30 sec, then on). If F8 returns, refer out. Do not clean.
Critical Rule If a machine shows F1, F2, or F8 — do not clean it. Document and refer. These indicate refrigeration or electronic issues that cleaning won't fix and could mask.
No Error Code But Machine Not Making Ice

Check in order:

  1. Is machine in ICE mode?
  2. Is water supply valve fully open?
  3. Is the bin full? (Check bin level — sensor stops production when full)
  4. Has it been less than 20 minutes since restart?
  5. Is the water curtain properly seated?
Clearing Error Codes Most clear automatically when the underlying condition is resolved. Power cycle after fixing: off for 30 seconds, then back on. If code returns, the problem isn't resolved.

🔴 When to Refer Out ▼
Stop Immediately and Refer
  • ✕ Error codes F1, F2, or F8 that don't clear after one power cycle
  • ✕ Visible yellowish oily staining near compressor or lines (refrigerant leak — do not operate)
  • ✕ Any burning smell from inside the machine
  • ✕ Sparking or visible electrical damage
  • ✕ Compressor making grinding, knocking, or screeching sounds
  • ✕ Machine not producing ice 30+ minutes after correct restart
  • ✕ Visible cracks in the evaporator plate
Note and Recommend (Not Urgent)
  • ! Condenser coils visibly clogged with dust
  • ! Door gasket cracked, torn, or mold deeply embedded
  • ! Water inlet valve dripping continuously between cycles
  • ! Ice production noticeably below nameplate capacity
  • ! Ice quality poor after cleaning (hollow, cloudy, very small)
Script "Everything from a sanitation and compliance standpoint is complete and documented. I noticed [specific issue] while I was in there — that's a mechanical matter outside what I do. You'll want a refrigeration tech to look at that. I can refer you to someone if that's helpful."
📋 DCM Series — Nugget Dispenser ▼
  • • CLEAN button is separate and dedicated — not the same as KM WASH
  • • Press and hold CLEAN for 3 seconds until LED flashes
  • • Dispenser mechanism — do not attempt to disassemble it
  • • Clean the drip tray thoroughly — high contamination area
  • • Ice chute exit is the primary ATP swab location
  • • Discard first 2 production cycles (not just 1) after cleaning
📋 FM Series — Flaker ▼
Safety — Rotating Drum NEVER reach inside the machine while it is on. The rotating drum with scraper blade will cause serious injury.
  • • Must be fully powered off — no cleaning mode
  • • Rotating drum with scraper blade — NEVER reach inside while on
  • • Apply cleaner directly to drum with soft brush
  • • 3 clean water flushes minimum after cleaning (not 2)
  • • Discard first 5 minutes of production after restart
  • • Inspect scraper blade each visit — if visibly worn, refer out before cleaning. A damaged blade can put metal into the ice.
