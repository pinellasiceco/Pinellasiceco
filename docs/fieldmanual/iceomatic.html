ICE-O-MATIC

ICEU / ICEUS Cubers · GEM Nugget · GEMD Dispenser
⚡ Quick Facts ▼

Market share: ~15%

Found in: Convenience stores, quick-service restaurants, small cafeterias, some bars.

Key difference: Stainless or aluminum evaporators depending on model. Relatively simple controls — CLEAN button is typically prominent on front panel. GEM series nugget machines are very common in convenience stores (Sonic-style nugget ice). ICEUS models have an under-counter form factor.

📋 Identify This Machine ▼

ICEU series: Standard upright cuber, cube or dice ice. Model sticker inside front panel (lift and pull). "ICEU" prefix + number = approximate lbs/day.

ICEUS series: Under-counter model, same ice type. Lower profile. Cleaning procedure is identical but access may be tighter — allow extra time.

GEM series: Nugget ice machine. Round pellet-style ice. Very popular in convenience stores. Cleaning procedure has additional steps for nugget-specific components.

GEMD: GEM with built-in dispenser.

CLEAN Button Location

On most Ice-O-Matic models, look for a dedicated CLEAN button on the front panel or just inside the access door. It is typically clearly labeled. If you do not see it immediately, open the front access door — it is often just inside on the control panel.

🔵 Before You Start ▼
1
Identify the model series before touching anything — ICEU, ICEUS, GEM, or GEMD. This determines whether GEM additional steps apply.
2
Tell the owner ice production stops for 1.5–3 hours.
3
Locate the CLEAN button — front panel or just inside the access door.
4
Locate the water supply valve behind the machine. Have all supplies ready before opening anything.
🟡 Deep Clean — ICEU / ICEUS Series ▼
Phase 1 — Document Before Touching Anything
1
Open the front panel — lift and pull on most ICEU models. No tools needed.
2
Take photos before touching anything. Photograph:
  • Evaporator surface
  • Water trough/sump area
  • Bin walls — look for pink slime, black mold, mineral deposits
  • Curtain underside
  • Drain area and drain plug
3
Put your gloves on.
4
Take pre-clean ATP swabs — record all readings in the app before cleaning anything:
Swab 1 — Sump interior Z-pattern, firm pressure against sump floor and walls, 4 full seconds of contact. This is typically your highest reading. Show the owner the number.
Swab 2 — Curtain/splash guard underside Full surface of the underside — press into any texture or folds.
Swab 3 — Bin drain area Around the drain plug and nearby bin floor. Press firmly.
Swab 4 — Ice scoop handle Full circumference. Scoop handles are touched constantly and almost never sanitized by staff.
Swab 5 — Bin wall mid-height Firm pressure, Z-pattern.
5
Record all readings in the app. Photograph the luminometer display for any reading above 500 RLU.
Phase 2 — Prepare the Machine
6
Scoop all ice out of the bin and discard. Every piece. Do not leave any ice in the machine during cleaning.
7
Press and hold the CLEAN button for 3–5 seconds until the CLEAN indicator illuminates. Do not tap — hold it until you see the indicator change.
8
The machine will harvest any remaining ice from the evaporator. Wait for the drop — you will hear ice fall. Wait for it to finish completely.
9
Discard the harvested ice from the bin.
10
The machine drains the sump — wait for the drain to complete. Look into the sump and confirm it is empty before adding chemicals.
Phase 3 — First Cleaning Cycle
11
Mix your cleaning solution for a deep clean:
  • 4 oz Nu-Calgon Nickel-Safe Cleaner
  • 16 oz clean water
  • Mix in your bucket before adding to the machine
  • Make extra solution for manual cleaning (8 oz cleaner + 24 oz water total)
12
Pour the mixed cleaning solution into the sump.
13
Press CLEAN to start the circulation cycle. The pump will circulate the solution through the machine.
14
Set a timer for 20 minutes. Go to Phase 4 now.
Phase 4 — Manual Cleaning While Cycle Runs
Tip Work top to bottom. You have 20 minutes — use all of it.
15
Remove the curtain/splash guard — grips and pulls straight out, or lifts up (varies by model). Place it in diluted cleaning solution to soak.
16
Scrub the evaporator surface with a soft brush and cleaning solution. Use light pressure — you are helping the cleaner contact scale deposits, not scrubbing a floor.
17
Spray bin walls with cleaning solution. Scrub all four walls top to bottom. Pay attention to:
  • Corners where walls meet (biofilm concentrates in corners)
  • Pink or orange discoloration — Serratia marcescens, very common in Florida
  • Black spots — mold, scrub thoroughly
  • Waterline where ice sits
18
Scrub the bin floor — focus on the drain area where organic material concentrates.
19
Scrub the door gasket — get the brush into every fold. Mold grows inside the folds.
20
Scrub the ice chute — the passage where ice falls into the bin.
21
Remove the drain plug. Scrub the plug and the drain opening. Set plug aside — do not lose it.
22
Remove the air filter (bottom front or side depending on model). Rinse under running water until completely clear. Set aside to dry — do not reinstall wet.
23
Take the curtain/splash guard out of its soak. Scrub both sides with the brush and cleaning solution. Rinse thoroughly and inspect — scrub again if biofilm is still visible.
24
Wipe around the water inlet valve area and any visible water lines with cleaning solution on a cloth.
Phase 5 — Assess: Does It Need a Second Cleaning Cycle?
25
When the 20-minute timer goes off, check before deciding whether to proceed to rinsing:
  • Is there still visible scale on the sump floor or walls?
  • Is there still visible biofilm on the evaporator?
  • Is there still visible mold on any surface?
Important If YES to any of these: mix a fresh batch of cleaning solution at 1:4 concentration. Pour into sump. Press CLEAN to run a second 20-minute cycle. This is common on first visits and adds 20–30 minutes — do not skip it if scale or biofilm is still visible.

If NO to all three: proceed to Phase 6.

Phase 6 — Rinse Cycles
26
Reinstall the drain plug firmly before adding any rinse water. Push in firmly and tug to confirm it will not come out.
27
Add clean water to the sump. Run for 10 minutes. Drain completely.
28
Add clean water again. Run a second 10-minute rinse. Drain completely.
29
Add clean water again. Run a third 10-minute rinse. Drain completely.
Tip Three rinse cycles minimum on a deep clean. Water draining after the third rinse should be completely clear with no foam. If still cloudy or foamy, run a fourth rinse.
Phase 7 — Sanitizer Application
30
Mix Nu-Calgon No-Rinse Sanitizer: 0.5 oz per gallon of clean water. This is a NO-RINSE product. Do not rinse after applying — rinsing defeats the entire purpose.
31
Spray all internal surfaces until visibly wet:
  • All bin walls
  • Bin floor and drain area
  • Ice chute
  • Sump walls and floor
  • Evaporator surface
  • Both sides of curtain/splash guard
32
Pour 4–6 oz of mixed sanitizer solution directly into the sump.
33
DO NOT RINSE. Leave the sanitizer on every surface.
34
Air dry for 5 minutes. Use this time to clean up your work area and rinse your brush and bucket.
Phase 8 — Reassemble
35
Reinstall the curtain/splash guard. Confirm it is seated level with no gaps.
36
Reinstall the drain plug — push firmly and tug to confirm it is seated. If it comes out, the machine will drain improperly during operation.
37
Reinstall the air filter only when it is completely dry. A wet filter restricts airflow and causes overheating. When dry, slide or clip it back into position.
38
Close the access panel.
Phase 9 — Restart and Verify
39
Press the ICE button to return to normal operation.
40
Allow 15–20 minutes for startup before first harvest. You will hear the compressor running.
41
Discard the first harvest completely. Every piece. The first batch may contain residual cleaner.
42
Take post-clean ATP swabs:
Swab 1 — Sump interior Same location as pre-clean. Target: under 10 RLU.
Swab 2 — Evaporator surface Center of plate. Target: under 10 RLU.
Swab 3 — Bin wall Mid-height. Target: under 10 RLU.
43
If any reading is above 30 RLU: re-spray that surface with sanitizer, wait 5 minutes, retest. If still above 30 after second application — document and note it to the owner. A surface that won't come down may have pitting or damage harboring bacteria.
44
Take post-clean photos of the same locations as pre-clean.
45
Log everything in the app — all ATP readings, notes, photos.
46
Show the owner the before and after numbers. "Before we started, your ice machine water contact surface read [X] RLU. The FDA standard is 10. After our service it's at [Y]. That's a [Z]% reduction. Here's your compliance documentation."
🟡 GEM Series — Nugget Machine Additional Steps ▼
GEM Layout Note GEM machines have a different internal layout from cubers. The key additional cleaning area is the ice curtain at the dispense point and the interior nugget pathway. Complete the standard ICEU/ICEUS procedure first, then perform these additional steps.
1
Power off completely before accessing internal components.
2
Remove the ice chute exit cover — usually snaps off by pressing the tabs on both sides.
3
Clean all surfaces of the chute exit thoroughly. Nugget ice flows through this constantly and biofilm builds up quickly — this is your highest-risk contamination area on a GEM.
4
Wipe down the interior of the dispense chute with cleaning solution on a cloth. Reach in as far as the cloth will allow.
5
Inspect the curtain/flapper at the dispense exit. Mold grows on the underside — it is dark and damp. Scrub both sides of the curtain with brush and cleaning solution.
6
Rinse all chute components with clean water. Apply sanitizer to all surfaces before reassembly. No rinse after sanitizer.
7
Discard the first 2 production cycles after restart — not just 1. The chute interior takes longer to flush through.
8
Primary ATP swab on GEM: ice chute exit interior. Insert swab into chute and press against the surface for 4 full seconds. Target: under 10 RLU.

🔧 Common Problems ▼
Machine Not Entering Clean Mode
What it looks like

Pressing CLEAN button does nothing, or the machine continues operating normally.

What to do

Hold the CLEAN button for 3–5 full seconds. Do not tap — hold it. If still no response, check that the machine has ice to harvest first (the clean cycle often begins with a harvest). If the machine is empty and still won't enter clean mode, power cycle (off 30 seconds, then on) and retry.

Refer out if

Machine won't enter clean mode after power cycle.

Scale on Aluminum Evaporator (Certain ICEU Models)
What it looks like

White deposits on the evaporator — same appearance as stainless, but aluminum evaporators are more sensitive to cleaners.

Critical

Ice-O-Matic aluminum evaporators require Nickel-Safe cleaner specifically. Standard ice machine cleaners can damage aluminum. Confirm you are using Nu-Calgon 4287-08 (Nickel-Safe) before using on any evaporator.

What to do

Do not use extended soak times on aluminum — over 30 minutes can cause pitting. One cleaning cycle at 1:4 ratio with a gentle brush only.

Refer out if

Evaporator surface shows pitting or discoloration beyond normal mineral deposits.

GEM Not Dispensing Ice
What it looks like

Machine is making ice — you can hear it — but the dispenser does not release ice.

What to do

Check for an ice jam in the chute exit — particularly if a previous cleaning left moisture that refroze. Clear the chute. Check that the dispense curtain/flapper is moving freely and not stuck.

Refer out if

Motor sounds strained, or the dispenser mechanism appears mechanically stuck beyond an ice jam.

Heavy Biofilm in Bin (Convenience Store Locations)
What it is

High-traffic bins are touched constantly by staff and customers and are rarely cleaned between service visits.

What to do

Two cleaning cycles on first visit is common at these locations. Document with photos. For ongoing accounts — recommend the owner add a bin wipe-down to their weekly staff cleaning checklist.

Ice Quality Poor After Cleaning
What it means

Hollow, cloudy, or off-taste ice after a correct cleaning procedure usually indicates residual cleaner from insufficient rinsing, or a water quality issue.

What to do

Run additional rinse cycles (up to 5 total) and discard 2–3 harvests. If ice quality does not improve, refer for water quality assessment — may need filter replacement or water softener.

⚠️ Error Codes ▼

Ice-O-Matic uses LED indicators — red, amber, and green. Check which LEDs are lit and whether they are solid or flashing.

Indicator Meaning Your Action
Red LED — solid Water supply or refrigeration fault Check water valve and filter. Power cycle. If persists, refer out.
Red LED — flashing Harvest or freeze fault Refer out. Do not clean.
Amber LED — solid Scheduled maintenance reminder Your service satisfies this indicator.
All LEDs flashing System fault / control board Power cycle once. If persists, refer out.
Green LED — solid Normal operation Machine is fine. ✓
Red Flashing LED — Do Not Clean A red flashing LED indicates a harvest or refrigeration fault. Do not clean. Document the indicator status, photograph the panel, and refer out immediately.
Amber LED Amber is a maintenance reminder based on operating hours or elapsed time. Completing your cleaning service satisfies this indicator — it should clear after restart.

🔴 When to Refer Out ▼
Stop Immediately and Refer
  • ✕ Red flashing LED that doesn't clear after one power cycle
  • ✕ Any burning smell
  • ✕ Sparking or electrical damage
  • ✕ Compressor grinding, knocking, or screeching
  • ✕ Machine not producing ice 30+ minutes after correct restart
  • ✕ Refrigerant oil staining near compressor or lines
  • ✕ GEM dispenser motor sounds strained
Note and Recommend (Not Urgent)
  • ! Condenser coils heavily fouled with dust or grease
  • ! Door gasket cracked or mold deeply embedded in gasket material
  • ! Water inlet valve dripping continuously between cycles
  • ! Ice quality consistently poor after correct cleaning (water issue)
  • ! ICEUS model showing water pooling underneath — drain line may be partially blocked
Script "Everything from a sanitation and compliance standpoint is complete and documented. I noticed [specific issue] while I was in there — that's a mechanical matter outside what I do. You'll want a refrigeration tech to look at that. I can refer you to someone if that's helpful."
🟢 Routine Maintenance — ICEU / ICEUS Series ▼
1
Open front panel. Take photos. Take pre-clean ATP swabs (sump interior and curtain underside minimum). Record readings.
2
If any reading is above 400 RLU on a machine you've previously cleaned — upgrade to a deep clean.
3
Scoop and discard all ice from the bin.
4
Press and hold CLEAN button until indicator illuminates. Wait for harvest and complete sump drain.
5
Mix lighter cleaning solution: 2 oz cleaner per 16 oz water.
6
Pour into sump. Press CLEAN to start cycle. Set 20-minute timer.
7
While cycle runs:
  • Remove curtain — wipe both sides with cleaning solution cloth. Rinse. Set aside.
  • Wipe bin walls with cleaning solution cloth — focus on corners, any visible spots.
  • Wipe bin floor and drain area.
  • Wipe door gasket folds.
  • Wipe ice chute.
  • Remove air filter — rinse if dusty, check condition.
8
When cycle ends, drain.
9
Reinstall drain plug firmly. Run 2 rinse cycles — add clean water, run 10 minutes each, drain completely after each.
10
Apply sanitizer to all surfaces. No rinse. Air dry 5 minutes.
11
Reinstall curtain (confirm seated level), drain plug (tug to confirm), air filter (confirm dry). Close access panel.
12
Press ICE to return to normal operation. Wait for first harvest. Discard it.
13
Post-clean ATP: sump interior and evaporator. Record. Photograph. Log in app.
Filter Replacement If filter replacement is due: complete during Step 9 while rinse cycles run.
