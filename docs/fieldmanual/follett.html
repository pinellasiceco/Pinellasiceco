FOLLETT

7 Series · 15 Series · Symphony Plus
⚠️ Auger Injury Hazard — Read Before Touching This Machine The Follett auger rotates continuously during operation and can cause serious injury. ALWAYS power off completely and wait 2 full minutes for the auger to stop before reaching inside this machine. Confirm power is off before every internal access.

This is not a precaution — it is a requirement. Auger injuries are severe. Never bypass this step.
Die Plate O-Ring — Watch for Leaks The die plate o-ring is the most common reassembly issue on Follett. After reinstalling the die plate, power on and watch the die plate area for water leaks during the first 2 minutes of operation. If you see dripping — power off immediately, remove the die plate, check o-ring seating, reinstall. Do not operate a leaking machine.
Startup Time — 20–30 Minutes Follett machines take 20–30 minutes from power-on to first ice production. This is normal. Do not leave during startup — watch for die plate leaks and confirm the machine reaches normal operation before you go.
⚡ Quick Facts ▼

Market share: ~10%

Found in: Healthcare (hospitals, clinics, cafeterias), some hotels, some food service.

Key difference: Auger-based nugget/cubelet ice production. The auger rotates inside a freezing cylinder to produce nugget ice. The die plate at the exit point uses an o-ring seal — correct seating is critical. This machine is very different from cube ice machines — internal components require full power-off before any access. Symphony Plus models include a built-in dispenser and sanitary ice delivery.

📋 Identify This Machine ▼

7 Series: Smaller capacity, single auger. Common in smaller healthcare settings.

15 Series: Higher capacity, same auger principle. Found in larger cafeterias and hotels.

Symphony Plus: Fully enclosed dispenser unit with sanitary ice delivery. Drain tray cleaning is an additional required step at every visit.

HCC: High-capacity, counter-height unit.

What the Auger Looks Like

On all Follett models, the auger is visible through the top or through the access panel — it is a spiral metal component inside a tube or cylinder. The die plate is at the exit point of the auger, where ice exits the freezing cylinder. Model sticker is on the back panel or inside the front access door.

Ice Type

Chewblet/nugget — soft, chewable nugget ice. Very popular in healthcare settings for patients. Same soft nugget style as GEM series Ice-O-Matic.

🔵 Before You Start ▼
1
POWER OFF and wait 2 full minutes before touching any internal components. Confirm the auger has stopped — listen for complete silence from inside the machine.
2
Tell the owner ice production stops for 2–3 hours. Follett takes longer to restart than cube ice machines.
3
Locate the water supply valve behind or beside the machine.
4
Identify the die plate location — it is at the end of the freezing cylinder where ice exits. You will need to remove and reinstall it during cleaning.
5
Have all supplies ready, plus your phone for the die plate leak monitoring timer after restart.
🟡 Deep Clean — 7 Series and 15 Series ▼
Phase 1 — Document and ATP
1
POWER OFF. Wait 2 full minutes. Confirm the auger has stopped completely before opening the access panel.
2
Open the access panel.
3
Take photos before touching anything. Photograph:
  • The auger area and freezing cylinder
  • The die plate
  • Bin walls — look for pink slime, black mold, mineral buildup
  • Drain area
  • Ice chute exit
4
Put your gloves on.
5
Take pre-clean ATP swabs — record all readings before cleaning anything:
Swab 1 — Ice chute exit interior Primary location. Insert swab into the chute exit and press against the surface. 4 full seconds. All ice passes through here — this is the highest-priority surface on a Follett.
Swab 2 — Bin wall Mid-height, firm pressure, Z-pattern.
Swab 3 — Drain tray Press against all accessible surfaces.
Swab 4 — Scoop handle Full circumference. Touched constantly by staff.
Swab 5 — Die plate face Press swab against the exit surface of the die plate.
6
Record all readings in the app. Photograph luminometer display for any reading above 500 RLU.
Phase 2 — Disassemble for Cleaning
7
Confirm power is off and auger is fully stopped before reaching inside.
8
Place your bucket under the drain area to catch any water.
9
Remove the die plate — it typically unthreads or unclips from the end of the freezing cylinder. Note the o-ring position carefully — do not lose the o-ring. Set the die plate on a clean surface.
10
Carefully remove the auger — grasp the auger shaft and pull straight out of the freezing cylinder. It is heavy: 10–20 lbs on the 15 Series. Lay it flat on a clean surface.
11
Inspect the auger fins — they should be smooth and intact. Note any pitting, cracking, or damage. Photograph any damage before cleaning.
12
Inspect the inside of the freezing cylinder with your flashlight. Note the scale buildup level — this determines whether a second cleaning cycle will be needed.
Phase 3 — First Cleaning Cycle
13
Mix your cleaning solution:
  • 4 oz Nu-Calgon Nickel-Safe Cleaner
  • 16 oz clean water
  • Make additional solution for bin cleaning (8 oz cleaner + 24 oz water total)
14
Pour cleaning solution into the freezing cylinder (where the auger was). Also apply cleaning solution to the auger fins with your spray bottle or brush.
15
Set a timer for 20 minutes. Go to Phase 4 now while the cylinder soaks.
Phase 4 — Manual Cleaning While Cylinder Soaks
Tip Work top to bottom. You have 20 minutes — use all of it.
16
Scrub the auger fins with soft brush and cleaning solution. Work around all fins — scale builds up between them.
17
Scrub inside the freezing cylinder — use your brush to reach all interior surfaces. The brush should contact the full circumference of the cylinder wall.
18
Scrub bin walls — all four, top to bottom. Focus on:
  • Corners where walls meet
  • Pink or orange discoloration — Serratia marcescens, very common
  • Black spots — mold
  • Waterline and ice contact areas
19
Scrub bin floor and drain area — organic material concentrates around the drain.
20
Scrub the bin door and gasket. Get the brush into every fold of the gasket — mold grows inside the folds.
21
Scrub the ice chute interior thoroughly. This is a primary ATP location — all ice passes through here. Be very thorough. Use the brush and cleaning solution on every accessible surface.
22
Clean the die plate — scrub all surfaces with brush and cleaning solution. Inspect the o-ring groove carefully and clean it out.
23
Rinse the auger under running water. Inspect fins again after cleaning — any remaining scale should now be visible.
24
Remove the air filter if accessible. Rinse under running water until clear. Set aside to dry — do not reinstall wet.
Phase 5 — Assess: Does It Need More Cleaning?
25
When the 20-minute timer goes off, check before proceeding:
  • Is there still visible scale inside the freezing cylinder?
  • Is there still visible scale on the auger fins?
  • Is there still visible biofilm remaining anywhere?
Important If YES to any of these: apply additional cleaning solution directly to the cylinder interior and auger fins. Allow another 20 minutes of contact time before rinsing. This is common on first visits.

If NO to all: proceed to Phase 6.

Phase 6 — Rinse
26
Pour clean water into the freezing cylinder repeatedly — 3 pours minimum. Each time, let the water drain out before adding the next.
27
Rinse the auger under running water — full rinse of all fin surfaces.
28
Confirm water from the cylinder runs clear with no foam before proceeding.
Phase 7 — Sanitizer Application
29
Mix Nu-Calgon No-Rinse Sanitizer: 0.5 oz per gallon of clean water. This is a NO-RINSE product. Do not rinse after applying.
30
Spray sanitizer inside the freezing cylinder — coat all interior surfaces.
31
Spray sanitizer on all auger surfaces, all fins, the shaft.
32
Spray sanitizer on:
  • All bin walls
  • Bin floor and drain area
  • Ice chute interior
  • Die plate surfaces
33
DO NOT RINSE. Leave the sanitizer on every surface.
34
Air dry 5 minutes. Use this time to clean up your work area.
Phase 8 — Reassemble (Follow Order Exactly)
Critical — Reassembly Order The auger must be fully seated on the drive coupling before the die plate goes on. If the auger isn't properly engaged, the machine will run but produce no ice and the motor will strain. Follow this order exactly.
35
Reinstall the auger — lower it carefully into the freezing cylinder, confirm it seats on the drive coupling at the bottom. You should feel it engage. Do not force it.
36
Inspect the die plate o-ring — it must be fully seated in its groove with no twisting or kinking. If the o-ring is cracked, deformed, or shows obvious wear, replace it before proceeding.
37
Install the die plate — thread or clip into position. Hand-tight for o-ring types. Do not over-tighten.
38
Reinstall the air filter only when it is completely dry. When dry, clip or slide it back into position.
39
Close the access panel.
Phase 9 — Restart and Leak Watch (Critical)
Die Plate Leak Watch — Do Not Skip Stand and watch the die plate area for the full 2 minutes after power-on. If you see any dripping — power off immediately. Do not leave this machine unattended during the first 2 minutes of startup.
40
Restore water supply if you shut it off.
41
Power on the machine.
42
Stand and watch the die plate area for 2 full minutes. Set your phone timer now.
43
If you see ANY dripping at the die plate — power off immediately. Remove the die plate, re-check the o-ring seating, reinstall, and restart. Repeat the 2-minute watch.
44
If no leaks after 2 minutes — you are good. Startup will take 20–30 minutes total before first ice production. Do not leave until you confirm normal operation.
45
Discard the first 5 minutes of ice output, or the first full bin cycle.
46
Take post-clean ATP swabs:
Swab 1 — Ice chute exit interior Primary location. Target: under 10 RLU.
Swab 2 — Bin wall Mid-height. Target: under 10 RLU.
Swab 3 — Drain tray Target: under 10 RLU.
47
If any reading is above 30 RLU: re-spray that surface with sanitizer, wait 5 minutes, retest. If still above 30 after second application — document and note it to the owner.
48
Record all readings, take post-clean photos, log everything in the app. Show the owner before and after numbers.
🟢 Symphony Plus — Additional Steps ▼
Symphony Plus Note The Symphony Plus has a fully enclosed dispenser with sanitary ice delivery. Complete the standard 7/15 Series procedure first, then perform these additional steps at every visit.
1
Clean the dispenser chute interior thoroughly — use your brush and cleaning solution on all accessible interior surfaces. Apply sanitizer before reassembly.
2
Remove the drain tray — it clips out from the bottom front. This is a high-contamination area. ATP test it separately as part of your pre-clean swabs.
3
Scrub the drain tray with cleaning solution on both sides. Rinse thoroughly. Apply sanitizer to both sides. Reinstall only after sanitizer has had 5 minutes of contact time.
4
Clean the dispense lever or paddle — it is touched constantly by users and is a high-risk contact surface. Wipe with cleaning solution, rinse, apply sanitizer.
5
Wipe all exterior surfaces of the dispenser housing — top, sides, and front panel. Use a clean cloth with mild cleaning solution.
6
After restart, test the dispense function — press the lever and confirm ice flows normally from the chute. Make sure the chute exit is clear and the flapper/curtain moves freely.

🔧 Common Problems ▼
Water Leaking at Die Plate After Restart
What it looks like

Water dripping from the joint where the die plate meets the freezing cylinder during startup.

What to do

Power off immediately. Remove the die plate. Inspect the o-ring — is it seated fully in the groove? Is it twisted or kinked? Cracked? If the o-ring is properly seated but still leaking, replace the o-ring (note the Follett model for the correct part). Reinstall and test again. Do not operate a leaking machine.

Refer out if

O-ring is replaced and still leaking — may be a machined surface issue requiring a service tech.

Auger Not Turning at Startup
What it looks like

Machine powers on and the compressor runs, but no ice production. Motor may sound strained.

What to do

Power off immediately. Confirm the auger is fully seated on the drive coupling. Scale can sometimes lock an auger in place — if the auger was not cleaned thoroughly, mineral deposits may be binding it inside the cylinder.

Refer out if

Auger is properly seated and clean but still won't turn. Do not run the machine.

Unusual Grinding or Clicking from Auger
What it looks like

Grinding, clicking, or irregular sounds from inside the cylinder during operation.

What to do

Power off immediately. This indicates auger fin wear or cylinder wall scale that the auger is scraping against during rotation.

Refer out

Always. Auger wear is a mechanical issue. Do not attempt to diagnose or continue operating the machine.

Ice Chute Exit High ATP After Cleaning
What it looks like

Post-clean swab at ice chute exit is still above 30 RLU after sanitizer application.

What to do

The ice chute exit is the most critical surface on a Follett — all ice passes through it. Re-apply sanitizer and wait 10 minutes (extended contact time). Retest. If still above 30, disassemble the chute exit further if accessible and re-scrub before re-applying sanitizer.

Note to owner

If reading stays above 30 after repeated treatment, recommend inspecting the chute exit for cracks or surface damage that may be harboring bacteria.

Low Ice Production
What it looks like

Machine is running and producing some ice but significantly less than expected based on model capacity.

What to do

Check for scale in the freezing cylinder — heavy scale reduces the effective interior diameter and lowers output. A deep clean with extended soak time may restore capacity. Check water supply pressure and filter condition.

Refer out if

Scale is clear, water supply is good, and production is still well below nameplate capacity.

⚠️ Error Codes ▼

Follett uses LED indicators. Location varies by model — usually on the front panel or visible inside the access door.

Indicator Meaning Your Action
Green — solid Normal operation Machine is fine. ✓
Green — flashing Startup cycle in progress Wait 20–30 minutes. This is normal.
Amber — solid Cleaning reminder Your service satisfies this indicator.
Red — solid Water supply fault or general fault Check water valve and filter. Power cycle once. If persists, refer out.
Red — flashing System fault Refer out. Do not clean.
Red Flashing LED — Always Refer Out A red flashing LED is always a refer-out. Do not clean or attempt to diagnose. Document the indicator status, photograph the panel, and refer out.
Green Flashing Is Normal Green flashing simply means the machine is going through its startup ice-making cycle. This is normal and takes 20–30 minutes. Do not leave until you see solid green and confirm ice is being produced.

🔴 When to Refer Out ▼
Stop Immediately and Refer
  • ✕ Water leaking at die plate that does not resolve after o-ring re-check and reinstall
  • ✕ Auger not turning (motor strained)
  • ✕ Grinding, clicking, or unusual sounds from auger during operation
  • ✕ Any burning smell
  • ✕ Sparking or electrical damage
  • ✕ Red flashing LED
  • ✕ Machine not producing ice 30+ minutes after correct startup
  • ✕ Refrigerant oil staining near compressor or lines
Note and Recommend (Not Urgent)
  • ! O-ring cracked or deformed — recommend having it replaced before next service
  • ! Auger fins showing pitting or early surface wear
  • ! Ice chute exit showing cracks or surface damage
  • ! Water inlet valve dripping continuously between cycles
  • ! Ice quality consistently poor despite correct cleaning procedure
